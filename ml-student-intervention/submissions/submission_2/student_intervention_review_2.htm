Training and Evaluating Models

Terrific discussion of the models and why you chose them â€” you added some great applications of the models, and formatted your answers nicely. Well done!

There are many issues to consider in performing model selection, and it's not always easy to know the best algorithm to use. It's often a good idea to at least try out simpler methods like logistic regression as a benchmark, and then move on to other approaches such as SVM, Decision Trees, and Ensemble methods.

(1) Small data
A big issue when justifying our model choice is the small amount of data. This should make training time with more complex models like SVM and adaboost less of an issue. We also might have been well served using a high bias classifier like Naive Bayes or Logistic Regression.

(2) Interpretability
Model interpretability is another important factor to consider, and if we'd used a simple Decision Tree we could produce a model which can be interpreted by the school board.
(Naive Bayes and Logistic regression would also be good choices based on interpretability).

(3) Accuracy
In order to achieve highly predictive results in the first place though, it's likely that we'll need a non-linear classifier, and your approaches of KNN, adaboost, and SVM's may all be effective here. Let's hope they work well!
